The Nature of Light

In the early 20th century, the scientific community grappled with a profound contradiction. Light, previously understood as a continuous wave, began exhibiting behaviors that could only be explained if it were composed of discrete packets of energy.

These packets, later termed photons, introduced the concept of wave-particle duality. This shift fundamentally disrupted classical mechanics and laid the groundwork for modern quantum physics.
